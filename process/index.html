№ 01

— First weeks

Discovery

Before any drawing, a long walk through the rooms you already have.

We meet at the property, not at the studio. Maja walks the apartment or villa with you — sometimes for an hour, sometimes for an afternoon — and asks questions that have nothing to do with finishes. How do you cook on a Tuesday. Where does the morning sun land. Who in the house wakes up first. The brief that follows is built from these answers, not from a brochure.

By the end of Discovery, you will have a written brief, a realistic budget range, a proposed timeline, and an honest opinion on whether the studio is the right fit for your project. If we are not, we will say so — and recommend someone we trust.

Site walk · Brief · Budget range · Timeline · Honest fit assessment

№ 02

— Weeks four to ten

Drawing

The room becomes a plan. The plan becomes a material library you can touch.

Maja draws every plan herself. You will see the apartment from above, in section, in elevation — and then in physical samples laid out on a table at the studio. Tile next to wood next to stone next to fabric. Lighting fixtures held up to the chosen ceiling height. The house gets decided in this room, before a single wall comes down.

You approve in stages: layout first, then materials, then fixtures, then the final pricing. Nothing moves to construction until every line is signed. We change our minds in this phase, not the next one — that is the entire point of it.

Plans & sections · Material library · Fixture selection · Final budget · Signed approvals

“By the time the first wall comes down, every decision is already made. Construction is not where we improvise. Construction is where we build what we have already drawn — quietly, on time, by the same hands.”

— Maja Milič

№ 03

— Months three to nine

Construction

The walls come down. The same hands put the new ones up.

Demolition first, then structure, then the slow build back: plumbing rough-in, electrics, drywall, tiling, joinery, finishing. Every trade is in-house — Bogdan, Iurie, Yurii, Vlad — and the four of them have built every Maya’s Homes home for years. They know the standard by feel.

You will receive a written update every Friday: what happened this week, what is happening next week, what has changed, what has not. Maja walks the site at least twice a week and replies to every question herself. There is no project manager filtering between you and the work.

Demolition · Structure · Trades · Finishes · Weekly updates · Site visits welcome

№ 04

— The final week, and after

Handover

A key on a polished countertop. Optional turnkey: the lights on, the bed made.

The final week is for snags. Every door, every drawer, every joint, every fitting — checked, listed, fixed. You walk the home with Maja and sign off room by room. The optional turnkey service (Service № 05) hands you a finished home, not a finished shell — furniture in place, lighting installed, soft goods made, the kettle filled.

And then we stay. A first-year tune-up is included in every project. After that, the studio remains on the call: a bulb in year three, a hinge in year five, a tile that needs replacing in year seven. The four hands that built your home are the four hands you call when something needs attention.

Snag list · Final walkthrough · Optional turnkey · Year-one tune-up · Aftercare

— What stays the same

The four phases run roughly in this order, but the rule that holds them together is older than any of them: the hand that draws is the hand that lays, and the studio that builds your home is the studio that stays on the call when something needs attention. Years from now, the person who answers your message will still be Maja. That is the whole design.
