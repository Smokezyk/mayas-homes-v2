— What to include

The most useful first messages tell us where the property is, what state it is in (apartment, villa, listed, just-bought, mid-renovation), roughly when you’d like to start, and what you are hoping the home will feel like when it is finished. None of this is required — a single sentence is also a fine first message.

Your message goes directly to Maja. Nothing is added to a list, nothing is shared.

— A short FAQ

Things people ask before they write.

How quickly does Maja reply?
Usually within the week. Sometimes the same day, sometimes on a Sunday — never by an autoresponder.
Do you take projects outside Cascais and Estoril?
Rarely. The studio’s quality depends on Maja and the in-house team being on site weekly. Anything more than thirty minutes from Alcabideche stretches that, and we usually decline.
What does a project cost?
Costs are quoted privately, after a site walk. We do not publish a price list — every home is different, and a quote made before seeing the room is a quote made wrong. Most clients receive a written estimate within ten days of their first meeting.
Do you work with international buyers who haven’t bought yet?
Often. The pre-purchase advice service (Service Nº 06) was built for this — Maja walks the property with you, before contracts, and tells you honestly whether the home is worth the work.
Which languages does Maja speak?
English, Portuguese, Italian, French, Spanish, German, and Slovenian. Write in whichever is easiest.

— The Studio

Visit in Alcabideche.

The studio is a short drive inland from Cascais, in Alcabideche. The material library lives here — tile, stone, timber, fabric, lighting fixtures laid out on long tables, available for clients to handle. Visits are by appointment, usually on a Tuesday or Wednesday afternoon over coffee.
