— How we work

Four steps. One signature.

Every renovation runs the same way: a conversation, a drawing, a build, and a key.

  1. Inspection. We walk the property with you and note what stays, what changes.

    01

    Week 0

    The Conversation

    A first meeting — no clipboards, no quotation. Maja walks the apartment with you, in your language, and listens. Every drawing starts here.

    “I want to know how you live before I draw a line. We keep designing all the way to the keys.”

    — Maja Milič

  2. Design. Every plan is drawn and rendered in 3D before a wall comes down.

    02

    Weeks 1–3

    The Drawing

    Every wall, every fitting, every finish — drawn, rendered in 3D, and signed off before a tool is lifted. What you see on screen is what you’ll walk into. No surprise costs.

    “We draw exactly how it’s going to be. Not a vision — a specification. Every line of it is yours.”

    — Maja Milič

  3. The build. Demolition first, then back up to the finished core.

    03

    Weeks 4–12

    The Build

    Our craftspeople. One site. One foreman. We move in. Nobody else does. Weekly walk-throughs in your language. WhatsApp updates if you’re abroad. The render becomes a room.

    “You can change a wall at any time. The build doesn’t close the design — it keeps the conversation going.”

    — Maja Milič

  4. Handover. Full inspection, snags cleared, keys to a finished home.

    04

    Final week

    The Handover

    A key on a polished countertop. The apartment is yours, ready to live in. Optional turnkey: furniture, lighting, and dressing included.

    “And we stay on the call. A bulb in year three is still our problem.”

    — Maja Milič

— Local mastery

A Cascais practice.

Where we work.

Maya’s Homes is based in Cascais, but our work runs the corridor — from the Atlantic cliffs through Sintra and Estoril to the historic quarters of Lisbon. Below: where we work. Click through to see how we know it.

The territory we work. The region we serve — every project inside this line. Three jurisdictions. Three permitting regimes. The Atlantic edge — what the coast does to a building.

Cascais, Sintra, Estoril, Lisbon — and the towns between. Click through to see how each is different, and why the differences matter.

Every project we take on sits within a thirty-to-forty-minute drive of Cascais — from Sintra in the north to Lisbon in the east. Crews on site daily, materials sourced locally, and Maja in person on every brief. This is the territory we work, and the territory we know.

Cascais’s municipal heritage office, Sintra’s UNESCO conservation rules, Lisbon’s Pombaline quarter — three towns, three permitting regimes, three sets of façade laws and material requirements. We’ve submitted successfully in all three, and we know exactly where they differ.

Atlantic salt, the weight of a Portuguese summer, the way a winter storm finds every imperfect seal — Cascais is a coastal climate with its own demands. We design and detail for it: marine-grade joinery, breathable plasters, sealants chosen for the air this house actually breathes.

“After seven years here, you stop being someone who works in Cascais and start being someone who is from it. That’s when you can build for it properly.”

“I know every road between Cascais and Lisbon by heart. The shortcut through Estoril when the A5 is jammed, the back way to a stoneyard in Marvila — that’s the kind of knowledge you only build by being here every day.”

“Every town hall has its rhythm. Cascais wants the drawings a certain way; Sintra wants every original detail catalogued before you touch it; Lisbon’s Pombaline office cares about the timber. After seven years, I know what each of them will say before they say it.”

“Portuguese weather is unforgiving — the Atlantic salt, the winter rain, the summer sun, the humidity between them. I know the market: which materials hold, which suppliers to call, and what every choice will look like in year ten.”

— Maja Milič

— What we offer

The design is yours. The standard is ours.

Every renovation is shaped by the client — your taste, your way of living, the home you want. The build is shaped by us. We use only our own materials and our own trusted suppliers, because that’s the only way to guarantee how the work performs in year ten.

Full home renovations

Apartments, lofts, villas, restaurant-to-residence conversions. Every wall, every fitting, every finish drawn first, costed second, built third — by one team, with our own materials and trades.

Kitchens & bathrooms

The two rooms that decide whether a home feels considered. Custom joinery, hand-laid stone, fittings selected for salt-air longevity. Often commissioned alone, often the start of a fuller renovation.

Structural & permits

Walls moved. Beams added. Engineering signed and stamped. IMPIC, AMI, Cascais and Sintra preservation permits handled in-house, in your language.

Property finding & turnkey relocation

For clients buying in Cascais from abroad. We find the property, manage the purchase, then renovate, furnish, and inspect — so the keys we hand you open a home that’s already ready to live in.

Pet-friendly homes

Homes designed with the whole household in mind, including the pets. Durable finishes, integrated nooks, materials that won’t show daily wear — all woven into the design, not added on.

Innovative materials & imports

Sourcing beyond the local market when the project calls for it. Emerging finishes, imports of high-quality materials from abroad, technologies not yet widely available in Portugal — all vetted through our own trades before any project sees them.
